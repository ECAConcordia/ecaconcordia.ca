CONCORDIA ENGINEERING COMPETITIONS

Welcome all to Concordia University’s 2016 Engineering Week hosted by the Engineering and Computer Science Association! As part of this special time of the year, we organized 6 super special competitions for ALL ENCS students. There will be cash prizes and a special ceremony to congratulate the winners! All participants receive a certificate along with an exclusive goody bag.

For more info contact Dilani Abeywickrama on academic@ecaconcordia.ca

Junior Design

Description:

Students are given a project to be completed in the allotted time on the day of the competition. They must create a solution and build an appropriate prototype of their proposed solution. Upon completion teams will be asked to demonstrate the usefulness of their solution by testing their prototype in front of an audience.

Evaluation Criteria: Oral presentation, quality of the report, originality of the design and prototype, respect of design constraints, and the performance of the prototype.

Eligibility: This competition is reserved to participants who have accumulated less than 60 credits towards their bachelor’s degree.

Number of Participants : Students may register as teams of 4, or individually. Individual sign ups will be placed in teams by the ECA.

Duration: 30 minutes of Briefing and Problem statement + 4 hours of Designing/Building Time [Tuesday 8th March 4PM - 8.30PM] 15 minutes Presentation + Testing [Wednesday 8th March, between 8 - 11AM]

Reengineering

Description:

Goal of the reengineering competition is to test participant's ability to make existing products better in order to answer new needs. The reengineered design will be presented to a jury allowing the participants to demonstrate their realistic imagination, technical and oratory skills.

Evaluation Criteria: Oral presentation, the quality of the report, originality, team spirit, and the effectiveness and feasibility of the system.

Eligibility: Open to all students

Number of Participants : Students may register as teams of 2, or individually. Individual sign ups will be placed in teams by the ECA.

Duration: 30 minutes of Briefing and Problem statement + 4 hours of Reengineering [Monday 7th March 4PM - 8.30PM] 15 minutes Presentation [Tuesday 8th March between 8AM - 10.30AM]

Consulting Engineering

Description:

Participants will be placed in teams of 4 and will be given a problem to solve during 4 hours. You will have to make use of your technical knowledge and innovative mind in addition to your understanding of the impact of engineering on our society, economy, environment and culture to solve the case.
During the competition, your team will be required to do research on a specific problem, synthesize your findings and develop a recommendation to be communicated to an audience.

Evaluation Criteria: Feasibility of your recommendation, Structure and quality of your presentation, Public-speaking and ability to persuade an audience.

Eligibility: All students enrolled in an engineering or computer science program in Concordia University.

Number of Participants : Students may register as teams of 4 or individually. Individual sign-ups will be placed in a team by the ECA prior to the competition.

Duration: 30 minutes of Briefing and Problem statement + 4 hours of Research and Preparation [Wednesday 9th March 4PM - 8.30PM] 15 minutes Presentation [Thursday 10th March, between 8AM - 11AM]

Scientific Communication

Description:

The often complex work of engineers forces them to rely on abstract and intangible concepts. This is why they must know how to simplify and communicate their knowledge in a clear and concise way with the general public. This competition is designed to force its participants to demonstrate their communicative skills by clearly explaining the operation of a device/process or a complex engineering subject.

Evaluation Criteria: Quality of their research, participants’ ability to explain complex concepts, the interest raised in the audience, and the use of audiovisual equipment.

Eligibility: All students

Number of Participants : One or in teams of two

Duration: 20 minutes Presentation [Wednesday 9th March between 5.45PM - 9PM]

Participants prepare for this competition before hand, and must submit their presentation file (PPT) and a 200 word abstract 48 hours prior to the competition.

Programming Challenge

Description:

Hackers unite! We are looking for the brave, the cunning, the hectic programmers out there! Come join us for an intense four hours of non-stop Extreme Programming! Challengers will have to team up in teams of two hackers and code on a single computer in hopes of solving complex algorithmic problems using the language of their choice. You think you can make the cut? Show us how it’s done!

Evaluation Criteria: Submitted algorithm must compile and will be given points based on how many good output they produce based on received input. In case of a tie, the execution time will be used as the base to determine the winners.

Eligibility: This competition is open to all engineering undergraduate students, no matter their field of study. A strong software background, especially in high-level languages, algorithms, and data structures will be of great use.

Number of Participants : Students may register as teams of 2 or individually. Individual sign ups will be placed in teams by the ECA.

Duration: 4 hours of Extreme Programming + 30 Minutes Break [Monday 7th March 5.30PM - 10PM]
